    🔒Privacy & safety

    When enabled, every ChatGPT conversation you open is parsed in the background so it's ready when you open the popup. Turn this off to only capture when you click Refresh.
    Re-capture the active tab at this interval while the popup or full-page dashboard is open. Choose Off to only refresh manually.
    Cap for the History tab. Older comparisons beyond this count are dropped from local storage.
    Cap for the Viewing capture picker on the ChatGPT and Google tabs. Older captures beyond this count are dropped.
    Clear archived captures Empties the picker archive only — the currently displayed capture stays in view until you refresh.
    • ChatGPT conversations are parsed locally on this device. When auto-capture is on, parsing happens as soon as the conversation tab finishes loading; when off, nothing is read until you open this popup and click Refresh.
    • Search pages (Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo) are captured either from the currently open search tab, or when you use the Open Google for prompt handoff — in that flow the service worker auto-captures the SERP after the Google tab finishes loading.
    • Stored locally on this device: the latest single-slot ChatGPT / SERP snapshot, plus a timestamped archive of every capture (the picker reads from this).
    • Sends nothing off-device. No analytics. No cloud sync.

    Note: “Search origin” is inferred from visible search queries, citations, and saved conversation metadata. Some live tool calls may not be serialized into the payload, so hidden retrieval steps can still be missed.

    Google Mode A: Search capture is heuristic and may miss or misclassify results if Google changes page markup.
